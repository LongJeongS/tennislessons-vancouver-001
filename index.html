Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do tennis lessons cost in Vancouver?+
Private lessons in Vancouver typically range from $65 to $95 per hour depending on coach experience and venue. Semi-private and group classes are more cost-effective at roughly $40–$55 per person per hour. We confirm the exact rate with your matched coach before you book, so there are no surprises.
Is it too late to learn tennis as an adult?+
No — tennis is one of the most age-friendly sports to start. Our adult beginner clinic regulars range from their late twenties to their mid-sixties, and many had never picked up a racket before signing up. The clinic is built around fundamentals, not athletic ability, so prior experience and current fitness aren't barriers to getting started.
Should I start with private or group tennis lessons?+
Both work, and the right choice depends on what you want from tennis. Private lessons get you to specific technique goals faster because every minute on the court is on you. Group classes are more affordable and build a community of players at your level. Many students do both: group classes for regular play, plus occasional private lessons when a specific stroke needs work.
How many tennis lessons do I need to actually play?+
Most adult beginners can keep a comfortable rally going after six to ten lessons. To play casual matches with friends, eight to twelve lessons over a season is a realistic benchmark. Your coach will give you a more specific estimate after your first lesson based on where you're starting.
Do I need to bring my own racket to the first lesson?+
Not necessarily. Most coaches keep loaner rackets for first-timers, so you can try a few before deciding what to buy. If you already own a racket, bring it. If not, mention it on the booking form and we'll make sure a loaner is ready when you arrive.
Where do tennis lessons take place in Vancouver?+
Most outdoor lessons happen at familiar Vancouver Park Board courts — Stanley Park, Kits Beach, Jericho Beach, Queen Elizabeth Park, and UBC. For lessons in Burnaby, Richmond, North Van, and West Van, we use the nearest public courts to your neighbourhood. Indoor facilities are available for the winter months.
Are tennis lessons available year-round in Vancouver's rainy season?+
Yes. Outdoor lessons run year-round when courts are dry, and we move to indoor facilities in Burnaby and Richmond when Vancouver's wet stretches make outdoor play unrealistic. Your coach will reschedule any rained-out lesson within the same week — you don't lose the session.
What is a Tennis Canada TPA certified coach?+
Tennis Professionals Association (TPA) certification is the national standard for tennis coaching in Canada. Coaches complete progressive levels of training in technique, drill design, communication, and safety. Every coach in our network holds at least a current TPA certification — and those working with juniors also carry a Safe Sport Tennis Certificate.
